Pork Ribs

Ingredients

  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • ½ cup fajita seasoning (such as Fiesta®)
  • 2 tablespoons Hungarian sweet paprika
  • 2 racks pork spareribs, fat trimmed
  • 1 cup beer
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 3 tablespoons Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tablespoon prepared brown mustard

Steps

Here is a Step by step guide to cook pork ribs.

Step 1

Gather all ingredients.

Step 2

Mix brown sugar, fajita seasoning, and paprika in a bowl.

Step 3

Rub both sides of pork spareribs with the brown sugar mixture.

Step 4

Place spareribs in a 9x13-inch baking pan; cover and refrigerate 8 hours or overnight.

Step 5

Preheat the oven to 250 degrees F (120 degrees C). Whisk together beer, garlic, honey,
Worcestershire sauce, and mustard in a bowl. Set aside.

Step 6

Tear off 2 large sheets of heavy-duty aluminum foil and lay them shiny-side down.
Place a rack of spare ribs on each sheet, meaty-side up.

Step 7

Tear off 2 more sheets of foil and place them on top of the spare ribs, shiny-side up.
Begin tightly folding the edges of the foil together to create a sealed packet.

Step 8

Just before sealing completely, divide beer mixture evenly into each packet.

Step 9

Complete the seal. Place the packets side-by-side on an 11x14-inch baking sheet.

Step 10

Bake ribs in the preheated oven until the ribs are very tender, 3 1/2 to 4 hours.

Step 11

Carefully open each packet and drain drippings into a saucepan.
You may only need the drippings from one packet.

Step 12

Place ribs on a foil-lined baking sheet and set aside.

Step 13

Heat the saucepan over medium-high and simmer drippings until the sauce begins to thicken, about 5 minutes.

Step 14

Brush the thickened sauce over ribs.

Step 15

Preheat the broiler and set the oven rack about 6 inches from the heat source.
Return ribs to the oven and broil until the sauce is lightly caramelized, 5 to 7 minutes.
